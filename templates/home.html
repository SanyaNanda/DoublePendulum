and it's motion

Kinematics of double Pendulum


Conventions
  • The origin is at the pivot point of the upper pendulum
  • y increases in upward direction
  • The upper pendulum is subscripted 1, and the lower is subscripted 2
  • The two pendulum masses as point particles

Let

x = horizontal position of pendulum mass
y = vertical position of pendulum mass
θ = angle of pendulum (counter-clockwise is positive)
L = length of rod (constant)
M= mass of the bob (constant)

By applying Trigonometry to write expressions for the positions x1, y1, x2, y2 in terms of the angles θ1, θ2 .

x1 = L1 sin θ1
y1 = −L1 cos θ1
x2 = x1 + L2 sin θ2
y2 = y1 − L2 cos θ2

The velocity is the derivative with respect to time of the position.

x1' = θ1' L1 cos θ1
y1' = θ1' L1 sin θ1
x2' = x1' + θ2' L2 cos θ2
y2' = y1' + θ2' L2 sin θ2

The acceleration is the second derivative.

x1'' = −θ1'2 L1 sin θ1 + θ1'' L1 cos θ1
y1'' = θ1'2 L1 cos θ1 + θ1'' L1 sin θ1
x2'' = x1'' − θ2'2 L2 sin θ2 + θ2'' L2 cos θ2
y2'' = y1'' + θ2'2 L2 cos θ2 + θ2'' L2 sin θ2

Dynamics of Double Pendulum

Let

T = tension in the rod
m = mass of pendulum
g = gravitational constant

In case of the upper pendulum, We reslove the forces in x and y direction.The net force on the mass is the sum of the forces in horizontal and vertical direction. Here we show the net force and use Newton's law F = m a .

m1 x1'' = −T1 sin θ1 + T2 sin θ2
m1 y1'' = T1 cos θ1 − T2 cos θ2 − m1 g

Likewise we apply the same concept on the lower pendulum and get the following result

m2 x2'' = −T2 sin θ2
m2 y2'' = T2 cos θ2 − m2 g

Equation of Motion

We apply algebraic manipulations on the equation derived earlier to get θ1'', θ2'' in terms of θ1, θ1', θ2, θ2' .

These are the equations of motion for the double pendulum.

Solving this double derivative equation w.r.t time we can calculate the angles θ1 and θ2.By solving the equation of motion in a range of time, we will obtain a range of angles and and there by predict the motion of the double pendulum. The Butterfly Effect

and it's analogy to double pendulum

In chaos theory, the butterfly effect is the sensitive dependence on initial conditions in which a small change in one state of a deterministic nonlinear system can result in large differences in a later state.The butterfly effect is the idea that small things can have non-linear impacts on a complex system. The concept is imagined with a butterfly flapping its wings and causing a typhoon.Of course, a single act like the butterfly flapping its wings cannot cause a typhoon. Small events can, however, serve as catalysts that act on starting conditions


Everything is connected with Everything Else

Famous words of an Ecologist, Barty Commoner can be understood in all realms of study due to presense of valid theories and in case of Physics, The Chaos Theory explains it well.

A small experiment to portray the idea behind the Butterfly Effect is shown in the Animation. Three Double Pendulums are brought into motion with minor changes in their intial state of position.For a while we see the pendulums moving in a more or less synchronised manner, but soon enough they start deviating from the common path and follow completely different ones.Thereby adding meaning to the definition of the Butterfly Effect!
